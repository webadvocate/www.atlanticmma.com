Ring of Combat 22--Ligouri, Bradley & Sachs Capture Titles

Posted in MMA Blog

Friday November 21st, 2008 – Atlantic City, New Jersey – The Tropicana Hotel and Casino played host to the 22nd installment of world Champion Louis Neglia’s Ring of Combat.

Many a promotion has crashed and burned over the past few years but the ROC remains a solid, stable and profitable MMA venture. Did you here that Mr. Shaw? – Profitable.

Elite XC, Bodog Fight, Mixed Fighting Championships, Cage Fury Fighting, Joe schmoe fighting league, Wamma Dabba do, Blah, blah, blah and they go on and on. Flash in the pan promotions that are very good at getting in to debt while trying to go head to head with the UFC or reinvent the sport.

All of the above would do well to take a page out Louis Neglia’s “How to run an MMA promotion” book. Bad business plans, bad management, short pockets, stupid new fighting mat bowl ideas – all are reasons why promotions crash and burn. Steady growth and establishing local talent has been the recipe for success here in New Jersey at the ROC. Many of today’s UFC title contenders & competitors have come up through the ROC; Pete Sell, Kurt Pellegrino, Frank Edgar, Jim & Dan Miller, Mike Massenzio just to name a few.

The demise of Elite XC benefited the ROC with the return of UFC vet and East Coast fixture Chris Ligouri as he faced off against Cory Mahon for the vacant ROC lightweight title.

Mahon was very aggressive in the first round and gave Ligouri some difficulty with his scrambles and counters but the momentum changed in the second round as Ligouri showed his experience and composure and worked his signature ground and pound from Mahon’s guard. The blows forced Mahon to scramble to escape the pounding which led to the armbar finish by Ligouri at the 3:41 mark of the second round, Ligouri finally secured the ROC lightweight title – persistence pays off.

Dante Rivera also made his return to the ROC- back from his brief stint in the UFC and was matched up against Lion heart MMA fighter Paul Bradley for the ROC middleweight title. Bradley scored the upset of the evening with a highlight reel left hook which sent Rivera down to the mat minutes in to the fight. Bradley seemed just as shocked at the turn of events as he pounced on the fallen Rivera to force the referee stoppage at :34 seconds in to the first round to secure the ROC middle weight title.

The only other title on the line was the ROC Featherweight Title contested between Tim Troxell and Dave Sachs. Sachs dominated this match up from the get go, he just seemed stronger and bigger than Troxell. I was stunned at the dominance as Troxell is one bad dude, but was out classed this evening (weight-class perhaps). Sachs controlled every aspect of the fight and eventually finished the fight with a modified rear naked/triangle choke at 4:40 R2 to secure the vacant ROC Featherweight Title.

Main Event Title Fights

ROC Middleweight Title
Dante Rivera (Ricardo Almeida) V. Paul Bradley (Lionheart MMA)
Winner: Paul Bradley wins via TKO at :34 R1.
Paul Bradley is the new ROC Featherweight Champion!

ROC Lightweight Title
Chris Ligouri (Ricardo Almeida) V. Cory Mahon (FCBJJ)
Winner: Chris Ligouri wins via armbar at 3:41 R2.
Chris Ligouri is the new ROC Lightweight Champion!

ROC Featherweight Title
Tim Troxell (AMA Fight Club/Renzo Gracie) V. Dave Sachs (Pittsburgh Fight Club)
Winner: Dave Sachs wins via Modified Rear naked choke at 4:40 R2.
Dave Sachs is the new ROC Featherweight Champion!

Main Card

Light Heavyweight
Constantino Phillipou (Team Serra Longo) V. John Doyle (Blackman MMA)
Winner: Constantino Phillipou wins via TKO at 3:05 of R3.
Doyle needs to spend more time working on his conditioning. He simply gassed out in this match. Another win for the Serra Longo rising star.

Welterweight
Igor Gracie (Team Renzo Gracie) V. Troy Maxwell (Extreme Performance MMA)
Winner: Igor Gracie wins via unanimous decision after 3 rounds.
Igor Gracie showed BJJ wizardry with one submission attempt after another but to Maxwell’s credit he managed to escape from some very grueling holds to survive the 3 rounds.

145lb.
Jimmie Rivera (TSMMA) V. Jay Mclean (Advanced Martial Arts)
Winner: Jason McLean wins via split decision after 3 rounds.
This was one of the most competitive matches of the evening – very close match but Mclean out hustled Rivera late in Round 3 to secure the decision.

238lb. Heavyweights
Joe Abouata (Alex Wilkie’s MA) V. Paul White (Rhino Fight Team)
Winner: Joe Abouata wins via rear naked choke at 3:06 R1.
Interesting fight while it lasted. White could be a force in the future with some more training.

Lightweight
Dom Stanco (Bellmore kickboxing) V. Biff Walizer (Lionheart MMA)
Winner: Dom Stanco wins via KO at 0:40 R2. Hard hitting Dom Stanco produced the knock out of the night with a perfectly timed left hook that put Walizer on dream street, watch out the Bellmore Bruiser.

Lightweight
John Salgado (Team Ross/Zocchi) V. Jay Isip (Team Kurt Pelligrino)
Winner: John Salgado wins via Rear naked choke at 3:24 R1.
Salgado has shown some serious growth over the last year and spoiled the return of Jay Isip with superior BJJ, a good win for Salgado.

Middleweight
Chris Presta (Bellmore Kickboxing) V. Mike Stewart (Jungle Gym)
Winne_r: Mike Stewart wins via side choke at 3:34 R1.
_Redemption for Stewart this evening, The TUF season 8 competitor was on the wrong end of Krystof Sosinsky’s haymakers during the first round of the TUF eliminations and suffered a TKO loss at the last ROC after dominating his opponent in the first round. Not tonight though as he executed his game plan and dominated Chris Presta with his wrestling to secure the submission win.

Preliminary Bouts

135lb.
Nick Pace (TSMMA) V. Sean Santella (AMA Fight Club/Renzo Gracie)
Winner: Nick Pace wins via Unanimous Decision after 2 Rounds.
A frantic “pace” led to the win for the TSMMA fighter.

235lb.
Pat Collins (AMA Fight Club/Renzo Gracie) V. Jim Boudourakis (Redboy BJJ)
Winner: Pat Collins wins via TKO at :31 R1.
“Patty boy” would have no part of Boudourakis’ grappling and stopped his takedown attempts and the fight with some very accurate striking.

Another night of exciting, well matched fights from promoter Louis Neglia!!
