8 Steps to Finishing the Kimura Submission from Guard

Posted in MMA Blog

I wrote a post the other day on The 3 Deadliest Mistakes Can Make In Your Opponent’s Guard based on an email from Stephan Kesting’s Beginning BJJ eCourse that inspired me to explore what happens to you when you make those mistakes.

The first mistake outlined was placing your hands on the mat instead of on your opponent.

What happens when you place your hands on the mat?

Well when you make the mistake of placing your hands on the mat instead of your opponent you open yourself up to a variety of armlocks.

One of which we will profile here.

The Kimura Lock from Closed Guard

There are various positions that you could obtain a kimura submission from, but we’ll highlight how to do it from closed guard when your opponent mistakenly puts his hand on the mat (lucky you) in this post.

Just follow and apply these 8 steps simultaneously:

  • You secure the wrist of the hand already on the mat
  • Open your guard
  • Escape your hips
  • Sit up to reach over the tricep with your free arm
  • Reach over and through his arm to grab your own wrist (making sure to keep his arm firmly against your chest)
  • Rotate your body til it’s perpendicular to his
  • Scissor his midsection with your legs
  • To finish the kimura lock you move his elbow towards his head and try to bring his hand to the back of his neck…he’ll tap way before it gets there.

Take a look at the “How to secure a kimura arm lock from closed guard” video that my instructor Morgan and I shot for you visual learners.
