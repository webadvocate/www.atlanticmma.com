UFC Fight Night 16: Fight for the Troops

Posted in MMA Blog

Josh Koscheck vs. Yoshiyuki Yoshida
Mike Crowley: Koscheck is on the rebound from his unanimous decision loss to Thiago Alves’ Muay Thai skills in UFC 90, so he’s definitely got the hunger for a win. Guys like him need to prove themselves. Alves beat him but it wasn’t a beat down, and I don’t think Yoshida has what Alves has, so, Koscheck by unanimous decision.
Matt Miller: I think Yoshida’s technical style will be a problem for Koscheck, as St. Pierre’s was at UFC 74. Yoshida will beat Koscheck.
Matt Scott: Yoshida will destroy Koscheck. There is a small chance that Koscheck can keep the fight standing and edge out a decision, but I predict Yoshida via Submission Round 2.

Mike Swick vs. Jonathan Goulet
Mike Crowley: Goulet doesn’t have the horsepower that Swick does, but Swick is coming off a recent surgery on his elbow. If Swick is healthy and at the top of his game. I don’t think Swick is going to want the fight to last, so he will want to wrap this up quickly. Swick by TKO late in the first round.
Matt Miller: I don’t necessarily think that Jonathan Goulet is a better fighter than Mike Swick can be, but at this point I believe that his experience will prevail. Goulet will beat Swick.
Matt Scott: Although I want to root for Goulet, Swick is far too dynamic of a fighter for him to handle. Swick has better standup, better wrestling, and better submissions. If Goulet wins, it will be on a mistake by Swick, not his own prowess. Swick via whatever he wants.

Jim Miller vs. Matt Wiman
Mike Crowley: Miller took this fight on very short notice. If Miller had time to prepare, he’d be my pick. In spite of Miller’s ability and potential, Wiman by split decision.
Matt Miller: Wiman has a few inches on Miller, and also a more disciplined style. Although Jim Miller has a better record, I think Matt Wiman is actually the better fighter. Wiman will beat Miller.
Matt Scott: As talented as Miller is, Matt Wiman has fought better, more experienced opponents. That, compounded with Miller taking it on short notice, means I’ll take Wiman via decision.

Steve Cantwell vs. Razak Al-Hussan
Mike Crowley: Al-Hussan is a potent threat to anyone who doesn’t take him seriously. He is going to have to tie up Cantwell to survive, and he’ll have the gas to hold him off for the first round, but not the second. Cantwell has the Muay Thai to hurt him and he will hurt him. After a couple heavy pops in the first round, Cantwell will find his mark in a second round TKO.
Matt Miller: I would like to do a better comparison on the Cantwell/Al-Hassan fight, but I really can’t because I haven’t seen anything from Razak Al-Hassan at all yet, I give the advantage to Cantwell simply on cage experience. Cantwell will beat Al-Hassan.
Matt Scott: Razak Al-Hassan is undefeated in his short MMA career, with no fight going to a decision. Fighting in front of a hostile crowd will be a new experience for him though, and it will be interesting to see how he handles it. That being said, I’ll still take Al-Hassan via Round 2 KO. And fight of the night.

Tim Credeur vs. Nate Loughran
Mike Crowley: This one’s a toss up. It could get exciting if one of them gets the upper hand on their feet. On the ground it’s a stalemate, but Loughran by split decision.
Matt Miller: Credeur and Loughran is a tough one to call, it should be a real even fight, so I’ll call Tim Credeur just on a hunch, and the notion that Nate should be getting cocky, with this being his 10th undefeated fight. Credeur will beat Loughran.
Matt Scott: I have Credeur controlling the standup, but Loughran dominating the ground game. Lucky for Tim, fights start standing up. I give this one to Credeur via Round 1 KO.

Preliminary Bouts:
Steve Bruno vs. Johnny Rees
Mike Crowley: Bruno is going to have his hands full, but his talent is what saves him against larger fighters. In this case Bruno’s ability will win out against a less experienced fighter. If Rees can last, he will lose by decision. Bruno by decision.
Matt Scott: I give Johnny Rees a slight edge just because of his desire to compete in the UFC. Not that all fighters don’t, but this kid really wants it. This should be a war, and Rees will have his hands full, but I predict Rees via split decision.

Luigi Fioravanti vs. Brodie Farber
Mike Crowley: Fioravanti is going to wear down Farber. Fioravanti is not an explosive fighter, but he hits hard and Farber will eventually succumb to the advance by TKO.
Matt Scott: Fioravanti has a huge advantage wherever the fight goes. Fioravanti via KO Round 1.

Eddie Sanchez vs. Justin McCully
Mike Crowley: Sanchez has chinks in his armour that McCully may be able to exploit on ground. If Sanchez can defend against McCully’s takedowns, it’s going to get ugly for McCully. When Sanchez isn’t tied up on the ground it’s hammer time. I think this one’s going to get ugly as McCully gets tired. Sanchez wins by third round TKO.
Matt Scott: If either of these fighters have cardio, it’s Justin McCully. I predict a relative snooze-fest until the third, where McCully will come on strong and force a referee stoppage.

Corey Hill vs. Dale Hartt
Mike Crowley: Hill is a stronger wrestler, and has the reach advantage not really weak to submission but unable to submit. Hartt is a heavy hitter, but not enought to neutralize Hill. Hill by decision.
Matt Scott: Hill is the better trained, more experienced, more talented fighter. Hill via decision.

Ben Saunders vs. Brandon Wolff
Mike Crowley: Saunders win by decision, maybe submission.
Matt Scott: Since this is a fight for the troops, I’ll go with the ex-Navy Seal on this one and take Brandon Wolff via Round 2 submission.
