UFC on Fox 4 Recap

Posted in MMA Blog

The UFC was on Fox for the 4th time and delivered a great night of fights. I imagine that both Fox and the UFC are very happy with the performances put on by all the fighters and we should expect the ratings numbers in the coming days. While going up against the Olympics is tough, the broadcast should have done as well as can be expected given the name recognition on the card.

Fight Results and Future Match-ups

Mike Swick made his triumphant return with somewhat of a come-from-behind win against DeMarques Johnson. Swick landed some good hard shots in the first that looked to have his opponent wobbled but Johnson rallied with takedowns and ground and pound that had Swick looking worn out coming into the second round. That however was where Swick was able to catch a body kick from Johnson and trip him to the mat, following up immediately with a hard right hand that scored the knockout. It was a great win for Swick as he gutted out some tough moments but came through victorious. Now that he’s shaken off the ring rust as well as broken a two fight skid before his injuries, I’d like to see him jump up a bit in the division taking on possibly the winner of Hardy vs Sadollah (understanding he lost to Hardy in 2009) or Matt Riddle who just won at UFC 149. Other possibilities for fun fights would be Matt Brown, the winner of Che Mills vs Duane Ludwig or Mike Pyle. Stephen Thompson would also be a fun matchup for Swick but coming off a loss makes it unlikely for them to be matched up at this point. Either way, Swick has always been an entertaining fighter and anyone willing to trade will make for an exciting fight.

Also preceding the main events, Joe Lauzon and Jamie Varner had an amazing fight. I was especially impressed with Varner who while visibly exhausted in the third round continued to push forward and throw hard strikes in combination and land takedowns. It wasn’t enough however as Lauzon continued to push the pace and locked in a beautiful triangle choke while transitioning from top position during a scramble, securing a Submission of the Night bonus and thanks to the gutsy Varner a Fight of the Night bonus as well. Those two awards brought Lauzons total to 11 bonuses in 13 UFC fights making him the current record holder. Chris Lytle formerly held the honor at 10 bonuses before retiring last year.

Lauzon not only won impressively over a tough opponent but he also put to rest some of the questions around cardio that came up during his 2010 fight with George Sotiropoulos. With his next 3 bouts ending in the first round, this is the first time we’ve seen Lauzon this far into a fight. He looked great, turning it up on the tired Varner to get the third round finish. This win puts Lauzon back into the contenders category in my opinion and I’d like to see him match up against the winner of Evan Dunham vs TJ Grant set to take place at UFC 152.

The Main and Co-Main Events

Lyoto “The Dragon” Machida vs Ryan “Darth” Bader

The Co-Main Event fight saw the evasive Lyoto Machida slipping and countering, frustrating Ryan Bader for the entire first round. In the second, Bader gave into that frustration loading up a big right hand that Machida saw coming. Blocking the incoming punch and landing his own counter at the same time; Machida scored his 7th knock out and a shot at the winner of Jones vs Henderson for the title. While Bader was completely out struck, I don’t think this loss does much to damage his career. Still in the top ten of the division, a fight with Brandon Vera seems appropriate as even in a loss, Vera looked much improved against Shogun, which brings us to our next fight.

Brandon Vera vs Mauricio “Shogun” Rua

Brandon Vera got his respect back in a spirited effort against Mauricio “Shogun” Rua. While Rua outclassed Vera positionally on the ground in the first, Vera never stopped firing hard combinations whenever he had the chance. Going into the second, Rua maintained his dominance, rocking Vera with knees and punches and looking close to finishing out the fight. Vera survived though and at not even 2 minutes into the second round, Rua started to look fatigued. Neither fighter stopped throwing and the two exchanged on the feet and on the ground into the fourth round. While Rua continued to look gassed, he was able to continue to win rounds, landing more strikes and getting a few takedowns before finishing with a TKO in the fourth.

During the press conference, one member of the media asked Shogun about his conditioning to which Vera angrily interjected that people were acting as though Rua gassed on his own and weren’t giving him recognition for pushing the pace. While I’ll readily admit that Vera was dishing out as often as he could, Rua looked tired barely 2 minutes into the second round and he was dominating the fight to that point. It’s possible he wore himself out trying to finish while Vera continued to fight on and I’m in no way taking anything away from Vera but given the pace of the fight Shogun should have had more in him. He should have been able to keep his momentum going into the second and kept more pressure on Vera. As a result of these performances, Machida was awarded the next title shot ahead of Shogun despite Shogun arguably having better fights since his title loss and Machida being only one win removed from his own loss.

From here, I’ve already noted that I’d like to see a bout between Bader and Vera to really separate the two and see who rises back towards the top of the division. For Shogun, the question is a bit more difficult. Having turned down a fight with Glover Teixiera earlier this year and given his performance against Vera, it seems Shogun is unwilling to fight some of the up-and-comers like Alexander Gustafsson or Teixeira, who I think would make suitable tests of where Shogun is at physically at this point in his career. Rashad Evans probably makes the most sense, especially since Shogun is one of the few elite Light Heavyweights that Rashad has not yet faced.

Overall it was an entertaining and compelling night of fights for the UFC and Fox. Given the first 3 Fox events were underwhelming as a whole, this was a great card and was encouraging that the UFC was willing to load up the names they did for a free broadcast.
